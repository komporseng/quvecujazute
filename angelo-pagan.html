    Angelo Pagan- Relationship, Married Life

    Pagan is married to actress Leah Remini; the two began dating in 1996 and married in 2003, with their wedding taking place at the Belagio Hotel in Las Vegas, which was featured in the VH1 special “Inside Out.” Sofia Bella, the couple’s daughter, was born in 2004.

    The couple recently made headlines after Remini revealed in her novel “Troublemaker: Surviving Hollywood and Scientology” that Pagan cheated on her numerous times during their relationship, but the two are still together today.

    Pagan was previously married and has three sons from that marriage.

    Angelo Pagan’s – Professional Career

    Pagan chose to relocate to California permanently when he reached 22 and pursue acting full-time. In 1989, he made his television debut as Luis in the series “Midnight Caller.” Following that, he worked on several projects, including roles in the television films “Kiss Shot, Sleep, Baby, Sleep” and “Virus.” His career and financial worth were developed during his early years as an actor.

    Pagan made his film debut in 1998, playing the character of a Cuban mailman in “Dance With Me,” after years of acting on television. After that, he returned to television and was cast in a variety of shows such as “Martial Law,” “Dharma & Greg,” and “Los Beltran,” but it was in 2001 that he received one of his biggest career breaks, when he joined the star-studded cast of “Swordfish,” playing Torres and starring alongside John Travolta, Hugh Jackman, and Halle Berry. The success of “Swordfish” aided his career and increased his income.

    Pagan also appeared in “24,” “Hawthorne,” and “In the Motherhood,” as well as six episodes of “The King of Queens” in various roles. In 2002, he appeared in the television film “Almost a Woman”; his various television and film productions all contributed to his net worth.

    Pagan is a musician in addition to being an actor. He used to be in the band Salsa Caliente, but he also recorded his solo album, “Don’t Be Afraid.”

    He is also renowned as the co-owner and manager of “Vivian’s Millennium Café,” a restaurant in Studio City, California that he co-owns with his wife’s stepfather, George Remini, and is famed for its pancakes, sandwiches, and Puerto Rican meals.
