    Who is John Lithgow?

    John Lithgow is a talented American stage and character actor. He is also a poet, singer, and musician. He is best known for his portrayal of Dick Solomon in the NBC comedy “3rd Rock from the Sun.” In the Netflix original historical drama online television ‘The Crown,’ he played Winston Churchill, the United Kingdom’s first Prime Minister.

    What is the Net Worth of John Lithgow? Salary, Earnings

    How much money does John Lithgow have? As a result of his many careers as an actor, musician, and author, the actor has amassed a net worth of around $45 million. He earned $75,000 for each episode of the comedic television series 3rd Rock from the Sun during the 1996-1997 season.

    Similarly, he was paid $200,000 per episode during the 1999-2000 season. He was the highest-paid actor in The Crown, despite having less screen time than the main character Claire Foy.

    Where was John Lithgow born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

    John Lithgow was born on October 19, 1945, into a theatrical family in Rochester, New York, USA. John Arthur Lithgow’s full name is John Arthur Lithgow. He is a mixed-race American of Welsh, English, Scots-Irish, Northern Irish, and Scottish ancestry. Arthur Washington Lithgow III (father) and Sarah Jane (mother) are his parents’ names.

    His father is a well-known producer and director who previously ran Princeton, New Jersey’s McCarter Theatre. Similarly, his mother was an actress in the past. He also has a younger brother named Robin Lithgow. In Princeton, New Jersey, John Lithgow attended Princeton High School. He earned a bachelor’s degree in history and literature from Harvard College in 1967.

    While at Harvard, he was heavily involved in student theater, acting, and directing. He made his Harvard debut in Gilbert and Sullivan’s Utopia Limited. Likewise, he was awarded a Fulbright Scholarship to attend the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art. Following graduation, he worked as the Director of the Arts and Literature Department at WBAI, the Pacifica radio station in New York City.
