    Michael is an accomplished swimmer who won eight gold medals at the Olympics in 2008, eight gold medals in 2004, and four gold medals in 2012. He surpassed all other Olympic athletes in accolades with 28 total medals earned throughout his career, including 23 golds.

    Michael inspires us with his quote, “You can’t put a limit on anything. The more you dream, the farther you get.”

    Michael Phelps Early Life:

    Michael was born on June 30, 1985, in Baltimore. When Michael was seven, he started swimming to burn his excess calories. At age ten, he was diagnosed with ADHD. He began taking his sport seriously after signing up with the North Baltimore Aquatic Club. 

    Michael once revealed that he consumed 12,000 calories per day during the 2008 Beijing Olympics and became the youngest American male to be selected to the national swim squad in 68 years.

    Michael Phelps Education:

    Michael attended Towson High School, Dumbarton Middle School, and Rodgers Forge Elementary School. He began his training with Bob Bowman when he was 11 years old. Bowman put the star through rigorous discipline during training. He studied sports management and marketing at the University of Michigan.

    Michael Phelps Family:

    Michael is of Welsh, English, German, Irish, and Scottish ancestry. His parents, Michael Fred Phelps and Debora Sue Phelps divorced when he was a little child. Michael grew up with his mother, Debbie, and sisters, Whitney and Hillary. His father, Fred, remarried in 2000 after his parent’s divorce in 1994.

    Michael Phelps Physical Stats:

    Michael stands tall, at 6 feet. He has an athletic build and a buffed-up physique and looks very charming. His body measures 46-34-16 inches. His height, large hands, and feet give him an edge to swim.

    Michael Phelps Relationship Status:

    Michael is married to Nicole Johnson, Miss California, USA. They began dating in 2007 and remained together until their breakup in 2012. A few years later, Phelps and Johnson rekindled, and in 2015, they became engaged.

    In May 2016, Michael and Nicole Johnson welcomed Boomer into the world. A month later, they tied the knot. In February 2018, they had their second son, Beckett Richard. Their third child, Maverick Nicolas, was born in September 2019.

    Michael Phelps Career:

    He came in fifth at the Sydney Olympic Games in the 200-meter butterfly in 2000. He set a new men’s swimming world record in the 2001 U.S. spring nationals when he clocked 1 minute, 54.92 seconds in the 200-meter butterfly at age 15.

    Michael Phelps Medals:

    Michael won six gold medals at the 2004 Olympic Games in Athens (200- and 400-meter individual medley, 100- and 200-meter butterfly, 4 x 200-meter freestyle relay, and 4 x 100-meter medley relay), two bronze medals (200- and 4 x 100-meter freestyle relay), and five Olympic or world records.

    Phelps entered the Beijing Olympics in 2008, hoping to surpass Spitz’s record of seven gold medals in a single Olympics. In his first three competitions—the 400-meter individual medley, the 4 x 100-meter freestyle relay, and the 200-meter freestyle—he won the gold medal, and each victory was accomplished in a world record time.

    Phelps got off to a bumpy start at the London Olympics in 2012, finishing last in his first event, the 400-meter individual medley. However, he won gold in the 4x 200-meter freestyle relay and silver in the 200-meter butterfly and 4x 100-meter freestyle relay.

    Michael concluded his Olympic career with an incredible 23 Gold medals, smashing the records set by Mark Spitz. 

    Phelps declared in April 2014 that he would return to competitive swimming; therefore, his retirement was only temporary. He again withdrew from competitive swimming after finishing his improbably successful comeback at the 2016 Games.
